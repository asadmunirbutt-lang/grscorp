## CONTENT EXTRACTION GUIDE

### Step 1: Get the Raw Content
From your HTML blog file, extract:
- `<h1>` or title → becomes article heading
- All `<h2>`, `<h3>` → keep as subheadings
- All `<p>` content → main body text
- Remove: CSS, scripts, navigation, footers

### Step 2: Add Medium-Specific Intro
Before main content, add 1-2 sentences explaining why this matters. Examples:

**For Educational Content:**
"If you're looking to understand [topic], this guide breaks down the science and practical applications in a way that makes sense."

**For Practical Guides:**
"Parents often ask me about [topic]. Here's what I've learned works best from working with families..."

**For Research/Genetics:**
"Recent research shows [key finding]. Here's what it means for individuals and families..."

### Step 3: Format for Medium
- Use Medium's native heading styles (H2, H3, H4)
- Break up long paragraphs (Medium readers prefer shorter paragraphs)
- Use bullet points liberally
- Add bold text to key points
- No images needed (but Medium supports them)

### Step 4: Add Outro + Backlink CTA
Use the template above with clear call-to-action linking back to GRSCorp.
